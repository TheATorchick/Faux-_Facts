Relevant Laws

Learn about laws relevant to animal rights and fur farms

United States Laws

The Animal Welfare Act

Sets minimum standards for housing, feeding, and care of some animals, but does not specifically regulate animals raised for fur like mink in a strong or detailed way.

Animal Enterprise Terrorism Act

Protects animal-related businesses (including fur farms) by making it illegal to interfere with their operations, such as releasing animals.

Wildlife Protection Act of 2019

Bans commercial trapping and sale of fur from wild animals in California, effectively limiting part of the fur trade.

European Laws

Fur Farming (Prohibition) Act 2000

Fully bans keeping animals solely for fur production in England and Wales.

EU Invasive Species Regulation

American mink are classified as invasive, meaning breeding and keeping them will be heavily restricted or banned across EU countries by 2027.
